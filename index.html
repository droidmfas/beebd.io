The Ringed Giant's Atmosphere

Exploring the dynamic storm systems, jet streams, and fluid mechanics of Saturn's upper atmosphere.

Zonal Jet Streams

Saturn boasts equatorial winds reaching velocities up to 1,800 km/h (1,100 mph), making them among the fastest atmospheric currents in the Solar System.

Great White Spots

Occurring roughly once every 30 Earth years, these massive planetary-scale storms erupt violently into the upper troposphere, encircling the globe.

Polar Vortices

Both poles host long-lived cyclonic systems with distinct eye-walls, reminiscent of terrestrial hurricanes on a massive cosmic scale.
